## Welcome

[Pico-8](https://www.lexaloffle.com/pico-8.php) is a "[fantasy console](https://www.lexaloffle.com/pico-8.php?page=faq)", a [virtual machine](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Virtual_machine) which aims to recreate the feeling of using a vintage [microcomputer](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Microcomputer), with modern features and conveniences. Pico-8's virtual [Audio Processing Unit](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Sound_chip) (vAPU) is capable of producing 4 channels of [monophonic](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Monophony) audio from a pre-defined selection of 8 basic waveform shapes (or user-provided [wavetables](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Wavetable_synthesis)), plus a single channel of [PCM audio](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Pulse-code_modulation). For sound designers and composers, a [music tracker](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Music_tracker) and visual editor are provided for programming audio playback data.

## Pre-Requisites

The goal of this series is to provide comprehensive knowledge in using Pico-8's audio tools to sound designers, from an audio production perspective. While this guide specifically targets Pico-8, many lessons should hopefully translate to other music trackers, sequencing programs, and various hardware platforms.

Previous experience with music composition and audio production tools is recommended, but not required. Additionally, some familiarity with [Pico-8-flavored Lua](https://www.lexaloffle.com/dl/docs/pico-8_manual.html#Lua_Syntax_Primer) is recommended, for lessons implementing runtime applications.

If you are new to Pico-8, music trackers, or music composition in general, you may want to visit the [Community Reference Material](/books/p8audio/resources/reference) page before continuing.
