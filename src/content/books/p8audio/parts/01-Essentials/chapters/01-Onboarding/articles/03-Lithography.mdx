## Key

Whenever possible, examples will be in the key of [C Major](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/C_major) (or a relative mode, like [A minor](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/A_minor)), using only the notes `C`, `D`, `E`, `F`, `G`, `A`, `B`.

## Engraving

![The Pico-8 sequence editor showing various subdivisions](/content/books/p8audio/01-03-subdivisions.png)

* Each sfx sequence used in this series will represent 2 "bars'" (aka "measures") worth of music.
* Each "bar" will be divided into 4 "beats".
* Each "beat" will be divided into 4 subdivisions, usually referred to as "[16th notes](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Sixteenth_note)", with each step of a sequence representing a single 16th note.
* Following the [American music counting system](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Counting_(music)) convention for 16th notes, we can refer to each individual subdivision as an extension of the beat it belongs to; eg, `1` ("one"), `1e` ("one-eee"), `1&` ("one-and"), `1a` ("one-ah"), `2` ("two"), `2e` ("two-eee"), etc.

![The Pico-8 sfx overview](/content/books/p8audio/01-03-1-chordprogressions_19.gif)

When possible, we will try to visually organize our sfx sequences so that they correspond with the patterns they've been assigned to. For example, pattern `00` will be assigned sfx `00`, `08`, `16`, and `24`. Pattern `01`, will be assigned sfx `01`, `08`, `16`, and `25`, etc.
